Projects

Facial Emotion Detection

Facial Emotion Detection

Detecting Emotions in a Remote Work Era using Facial Emotion Detection

Reduction in gatherings and social events, and the increase in remote meetings and work has impacted us, both positively and negatively. The reduced socialising and physical meetings may have affected the way how we’re able to read body languages and actions during conversations. Using OpenCV and Tensorflow, and testing with different pre-trained models with transfer learning (such as VGG16, Resnet50, etc), I’ve developed a model that achieves a 77% accuracy in detecting facial emotions based on the classes: ‘anger’, ‘contempt’, ‘disgust’, ‘fear’, ‘happiness’, ‘neutral’, ‘sadness’ and ‘surprise’.

This project also demonstrates some simple use in applications, using screen capture to mimic facial detection on video calls, and hosting a server for use on a web browser.

Classifying of subreddit posts

Subreddit Classification

Classifying titles of subreddit posts between r/Cryptocurrency and r/stocks

This project takes in posts from 2 subreddits in similar, yet different areas of focus, and attempts to classify the posts to the correct subreddit. In this case, stocks and cryptocurrency are mostly discussed as a form of financial tool for investment (except Cryptocurrency, which has a higher risk due to its volitility). Using vectorizers to detect word importance, various classification models were used here to find the best Area Under Curve(AUC) score.
